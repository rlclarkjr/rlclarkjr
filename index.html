Projects

LuMiRa: An Integrated Lung Deformation Atlas and 3D-CNN model of Infiltrates for COVID-19 Prognosis

Hypothesis of this study is that distensions induced in the lung by COVID-19 are a reflection of more advanced lung fibrosis or interstitial lung changes induced by more severe disease, and early characterization of these physiological based changes can portend the possible long-term sequela of COVID-19 and help in better prognosis and treatment planning for COVID-19 patients. Following are the key contributions of the study;

  • A new imaging biomarker using population atlas-based approach to analyze regions of lung shape distension differences between COVID-19 patients with mild and severe disease.
  • Integrating 3D-CNN characterization of lung deformation regions and lung infiltrate regions on lung CT into a novel framework (LuMiRa) for better identifying patients with severe disease.

Integrated Clinical and CT based Artificial Intelligence nomogram for predicting severity and need for ventilator support in COVID-19 patients

In this study we constructed an integrated DL and clinical parameter prognostic nomogram using routinely available blood parameters and standard-of-care CT scans at baseline in SARS-CoV-2 positive patients. The following steps were used to construct the integrated nomogram;

  • 2D U-Net based segmentation network for segmenting the ground glass opacities and consolidation regions in the lung.
  • An imaging AI predictor (AIP) constructed using 3D CNN to predict which COVID-19 patients would end up getting a ventilator.
  • Finally, the top 5 clinical features were integrated with the probability scores obtained by AIP and a multivariable logistic regression was trained to generate an integrated clinical and AI imaging nomogram (ClAIN).

An integrated deep learning, PI-RADS and clinical nomogram to identify clinically signficant prostate cancer on bi-parametric MRI

In this study, we constructed an integrated nomogram combining deep learning, PI-RADS score, and clinical variables (prostate-specific antigen, prostate volume, and lesion volume) using multivariable logistic regression to identify clinically significant prostate cancer on biparametric MRI. Based on the importance of the peritumoural region for characterisation of prostate cancer as suggested by previous studies, we explored multiple input configurations of the deep learning network, with patches extracted at different scales and each subsequent scale incorporating additional information from the peritumoural region.

Test-retest repeatability of a deep learning architecture in detecting and segmenting clinically significant prostate cancer on apparent diffusion coefficient (ADC) maps

A very unique study that focuses on the robustness and reproducibility of convolutional neural networks (CNN) rather than just on the diagnostic accuracy. This study consisted of MRI scans of prostate cancer patients which were taken just 15 min apart for each patient. If we train CNNs for specific tasks (in this case detection and segmentation of clinically significant prostate cancer lesions) on the first and second set of scans separately with exactly the same hyper-parameters, ideally they should produce the same or similar output. A multi-sequence multi-instance learning convolutional neural network (siNET) with combination of deep features extracted from intra- and peri- tumoral regions to distinguish clinically significant and insignificant prostate cancer on bi-parametric MRI

In this study we present multi-sequence multi-instance learning convolutional neural network (siNET) pre-trained by convolutional auto-encoders (CAEs) to distinguish clinically significant prostate cancer (csPCa) and clinically insignificant prostate cancer (ciPCa) on biparametric-MRI (bpMRI). We use siNET as a feature extractor and extract deep features from intra- and peri- regions and train random forest classifier to distinguish csPCa and ciPCa on bpMRI.

A 3D Augmentor module (Python)

Since it was difficult to find an existing python module to perform 3D augmentations, I created one by using SimpleITK's simple transformation functions. This is particularly important in the field of medical image analysis as most of the data we deal with is in 3D (MRI, CT etc). Multiple images and corresponding multiple segmentation masks can be simultaneously augmented using random transformations such as rotating, translation, shear, vertical and horizonal flipping. Environmental sound recognition using combination of spectrogram image and acoustic features

In this work we try to recognize different environmental sounds such as rain, sea waves, helicopter, chainsaw etc. using a machine learning classifier. Instead of just extracting representations from the time domain signal, we also extract features from the spectrogram image of the sound. We combine the spectrogram image features with the acoustic features and train a machine learning classifier to recognize different sounds.
